The TSA's 3-1-1 rule is one of the most misunderstood rules in air travel. Let's break it down clearly so you can get through security without losing your favorite shampoo.

## What Is the 3-1-1 Rule?

The 3-1-1 rule means:

- **3.4 oz (100 ml)** — Each liquid container must be 3.4 oz or less
- **1 quart-sized bag** — All liquids must fit in one clear, zip-top quart-sized bag
- **1 bag per passenger** — Only one quart-sized bag allowed per person

This applies to all liquids, aerosols, gels, creams, and pastes in your carry-on bag.

## What Counts as a Liquid?

The TSA definition of "liquids" is broader than you might think. It includes:

- Water, juice, coffee, alcohol
- Shampoo, conditioner, body wash
- Toothpaste, mouthwash
- Lotions, creams, sunscreen
- Mascara, lip gloss, foundation
- Peanut butter, jam, honey, yogurt (yes, really)
- Salsa, hummus, guacamole
- Aerosol sprays (deodorant, dry shampoo)
- Perfume and cologne

## What Doesn't Count as a Liquid?

These items are **not** subject to the liquids rule:

- Solid deodorant stick
- Solid lipstick
- Powder products (dry shampoo powder, protein powder)
- Hard candy, granola bars, solid food
- Fresh fruit and vegetables

## The 100 ml / 3.4 oz Limit

The container itself must be **labeled** as 100 ml or less. A 200 ml shampoo bottle that's only half full is **not permitted** — the container size matters, not how much is in it.

**Pro tip:** Buy travel-sized bottles or use empty TSA-approved travel containers you can refill from your larger bottles at home.

## Exceptions to the 3-1-1 Rule

### Medically Necessary Liquids
Prescription medications, over-the-counter medications, and medical equipment liquids are exempt from the 3-1-1 rule. They must be declared and screened separately. Keep medications in their original labeled containers.

### Baby Formula and Breast Milk
Formula, breast milk, and juice for infants are exempt. You can bring larger quantities in carry-on and they will be screened separately.

### Duty-Free Liquids
Liquids purchased at international duty-free shops after the security checkpoint can often exceed 100 ml in carry-on, as long as they are in a sealed, tamper-evident bag from the duty-free shop and you're traveling on an international flight.

## In Checked Luggage

There is **no 3-1-1 restriction for liquids in checked luggage**. You can pack full-size shampoo bottles, large containers of sunscreen, etc. in your checked bag.

However, IATA rules limit aerosols and flammable products (like perfume and hairspray) to 2 liters / 2 kg total in checked bags, with no single container over 500 ml.

## 10 Tips to Speed Through Security

1. **Place your liquids bag at the top of your carry-on** for easy removal
2. **One quart bag is often enough** for a week-long trip if you use travel sizes
3. **Buy toiletries at your destination** for longer trips
4. **Leave water bottles empty** — fill them after security from a fountain or café
5. **Solid alternatives exist** for shampoo, conditioner, moisturizer, and more
6. **Remove your liquids bag before reaching the conveyor** to save time
7. **TSA PreCheck members don't need to remove liquids** from their bags
8. **Declare medications** rather than keeping them hidden — it's faster
9. **Pack duplicates of essentials** in checked luggage in case carry-on is gate-checked
10. **Check airline and country rules** — some international security checkpoints have tighter limits

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can I bring a full-size bottle of water through security?**  
No. Full water bottles (any size) are not allowed through the TSA checkpoint in carry-on baggage. You can bring an empty bottle and fill it after security, or buy a bottle once you're past the checkpoint.

**What if my liquid container isn't labeled in ml or oz?**  
TSA officers may estimate the container size by sight. If it looks larger than 100 ml, they can confiscate it. Label your containers or use pre-measured travel containers.

**What happens if I accidentally have a liquid over 100 ml?**  
TSA officers will ask you to surrender the item. You can either leave it behind, put it in your checked bag (if you haven't checked in yet), or mail it to yourself. There are no fines for honest mistakes.
